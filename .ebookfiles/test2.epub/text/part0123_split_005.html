Glossary

urbanization
The movement of the population into towns and cities and away from rural areas.
ecological approach
In the field of urban analysis, a perspective emphasizing the “natural” distribution of city neighborhoods into areas having contrasting characteristics.
inner city
The areas composing the central neighborhoods of a city, as distinct from the suburbs. In many modern urban settings in the developed world, inner-city areas are subject to dilapidation and decay, with the more affluent residents having moved to outlying areas.
ghetto
A place where a racial or ethnic group initially comes to live as a consequence of systematic exclusion from more desirable places.
urbanism
A term used by Louis Wirth to denote distinctive characteristics of urban social life, such as its impersonality.
created environment
An environment made up of constructions established by human beings to serve their needs, derived from the use of human-made technology—including, for example, roads, railways, factories, offices, private homes, and other buildings.
collective consumption
A concept used by Manuel Castells to refer to processes of urban consumption—such as the buying and selling of property.
global city
A city, such as London, New York, or Tokyo, that has become an organizing center of the new global economy.
