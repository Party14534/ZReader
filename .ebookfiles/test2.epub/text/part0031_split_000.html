3 RESEARCH ON SOCIALIZATION TODAY: RACE SOCIALIZATION

Socialization research flourished in the 1970s, 1980s, and 1990s, as researchers explored the ways that social changes, like shifting gender roles, women’s widespread entry into the paid labor force, and increased reliance on paid child care, affected childhood socialization. Yet socialization research can help us understand other contemporary social issues, such as the ways that Black families cope with violence and prejudice, and how parents socialize children to respect individual differences in our increasingly diverse society.
