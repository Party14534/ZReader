Experiments

Experiments are often used in the natural sciences and psychology, as they are considered the best method for ascertaining causality, or the influence of a particular factor on a study’s outcome. In an experimental situation, the researcher directly controls the circumstances being studied. In a typical experiment, people are randomly assigned to two groups. The first, called an experimental group, receives some special attention based on the researcher’s theory; the second, the control group, does not receive this attention. The subjects usually do not know to which group they have been assigned and seldom know the purpose of the experiment, though this is not always the case.
